Osoyoos Lake
Come in, the waters fine

Osoyoos lake, Canada's warmest Lake, is split down the middle by the 49th parallel. The shores of which play host to twenty-two kilometers of beaches and parks. Despite all of this the lake never feels over developed with enough wilderness to satisfy even the most adventurous outdoorsman.

Landscape
Natural, world class beauty.

The very definition of picturesque, the Okanagan valley really is reminiscent of rural Italy. With rolling hills blanketed in vineyards and orchards for kilometers in every direction and the odd jagged cliff for contrast.

Osoyoos Lake is fed by the snow packs on the mountains to the east. Despite being fed by snow, the water temperatures routinely hit between 26°C and 33°C in the summer, making it Canada's warmest freshwater lake.

Water Sports
For the thrill seekers.

The combination of warm water & ideal conditions make it no surprise why lake Osoyoos has become a watersport hub. If watersports are not your thing, the lake is plenty big enough to find a quiet patch. If you are a thrill seeker, however, look no further than lake Osoyoos. Enjoy skiing, knee & wake boarding, surfing, parasailing and more.

Fishing
Sport and recreation

Fishing, for those with the patience it is nothing short of theraputic. Rarely is it more enjoyable than at Lake Osoyoos. The myriad of species supports all fishing styles. Whether you prefer the constant busy action of fly fishing or would rather cast once and crack a beer, Lake Osoyoos has you covered.

What species live in the lake?

  • Black Bullhead
  • Black Crappie
  • Carp
  • Coarse or "Non-Game" fish
  • Goldfish
  • Kokanee Salmon
  • Lake Whitefish
  • Largemouth Bass
  • Northern Pikeminnow
  • Pumpkinseed Sunfish
  • Rainbow Trout
  • Smallmouth Bass
  • Sockeye Salmon
  • Steelhead
  • Yellow Perch
  • Largescale Sucker
  • Mountain Whitefish
