Oroville, WA
Friendly & quiet.

On the southern-most tip of Lake Osoyoos lies the city of Oroville, an excellent retirement community and vacation destination. Origionally called Oro or "Gold" in Spanish, Oroville has a rich history of gold mining. The city is located in the west of the Okanagan Highlands, a region known for its rolling hills of forests and natural meadows. The Area surrounding Oriville includes the historic towns of Nighthawk, Molson & Chesaw.

Recreation
A small town with plenty to do.

Three local wineries along with a local brewery make for a classy vacation regardless of taste.

Public land in Oroville is far more common than private. This imbalance means ample room for exploration & recreation. The public property encompases everything from parks & campgrounds to the most untamed wilderness and everything in between.

Culture
Gold town

The city really embraces its name. Oroville, which literally means "Gold Town" for its rich gold mining culture, is a town that is proud of it's history.

The culture of Oroville can best be described as relaxed. An exceptional retirement community, the city is quiet, friendly and rural. Be it the wineries, the warm weather or both, the inhabitants welcoming personalities will make you feel at home in Oroville.
