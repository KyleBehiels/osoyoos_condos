Oroville, WA
Friendly & quiet.

On the southern-most tip of Lake Osoyoos, lies the city of Oroville, a relaxing retirement community and vacation destination. Originally called Oro or "gold" in Spanish, Oroville has a rich history of gold mining. The city is located in the west Okanagan Highlands, a region known for its rolling hills of forests and natural meadows. The area surrounding Oroville includes the historic and charming towns of Nighthawk, Molson & Chesaw.

Recreation
A small town with plenty to do.

Three local wineries along with a local brewery make for a tasty vacation regardless of preference.

Public land in Oroville is far more common than private. This imbalance means ample room for exploration & recreation. The public property encompases everything from parks & campgrounds to the most untamed wilderness and everything in between.

Culture
Gold town

The city really embraces its name. Oroville, which literally means "Gold Town" for its rich gold mining culture, is a town that is proud of its history.

The atmosphere of Oroville can be best described as relaxed. An exceptional retirement community, the city is quiet, friendly and rural. Be it the wineries, the warm weather or both, the inhabitant's welcoming personalities will make you feel at home in Oroville.

Weather
Beautiful year-round

Oroville, WA has one of the mildest climates in the United States. The Sperling comfort index rates it at 64, 12 points higher than the national average. With an average of only 26 days of precipitation a year, the sun is almost always shining in Oroville.

In the summer the average temperature is a comfortable 80°F. The warm weather combined with Canada's warmest lake, make for a truly pleasant experience.

Winter in Oroville averages around 18°F, cold enough to for a multitude of snow sports but not so cold as to be uncomfortable.
